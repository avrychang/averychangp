Granville Island - Redesign

Project Overview

This project was a design challenge aimed at testing our ability to analyze existing research and translate insights into a more effective and engaging website experience. The goal was to take the findings from the research phase and reimagine the Granville Island website, improving usability, navigation, and overall design while maintaining the brand’s unique character. 

My Role

UX/UI Designer

Graphic Designer

Team

Solo school project

Timeline

9 weeks

Tools

Figma

Challenge

The challenge was to analyze and synthesize the existing research, navigate through a large amount of data, and create a new or improved user flow that enhanced the overall website experience. This involved refining navigation, improving usability, and reworking the interface while ensuring that the design remained aligned with Granville Island’s branding. 

Approach

To thoroughly analyzing the collected data and applying my own design experience to address the identified challenges. I knew I wanted the website to feel fun, vibrant, and visually engaging. To achieve this, I focused on making better use of images to convey the essence of Granville Island while reducing the reliance on large chunks of text. This allowed for a more dynamic, user-friendly experience that captured the island’s lively atmosphere while ensuring easy navigation and accessibility. 

Design Principles and Recommendations

IMy redesign followed key user-centered design principles, ensuring a seamless and engaging experience. The focus is on consistency, clarity, and personalization, allowing both locals and tourists to navigate effortlessly. By using high-quality visuals, vibrant colors, and interactive elements, the design fosters engagement while maintaining brand identity. Recommendations include a personalized trip planner, an interactive map, streamlined content, and a refined site structure to enhance usability and efficiency.

User Persona

User Flow

Based off the research I decided enhance the user experience on Granville Island’s website, I revamped the personalized trip planner. Allowing users to easily create, customize, and share their own itineraries. This feature provides tailored suggestions based on user preferences, including interests, trip duration, and group type, making planning more intuitive and enjoyable. The ability to edit and share plans in real-time ensures a flexible and collaborative experience for both locals and tourists.

Wireframes

Based on the user flow I developed for the personalized trip planner, I created wireframes to better visualize and showcase the redesigned layout. These wireframes highlight a more intuitive structure, allowing users to easily create, customize, and share their trip plans.

Mockup

Building on the wireframes, I created two high-fidelity mockups—one for the homepage and another for a secondary page—to showcase the redesigned Granville Island website. Sticking to the island’s branding, I aimed for a modern, clean design with vibrant, fun colors to reflect Granville Island’s unique personality. I focused on simplifying the layout, reducing large blocks of text, and creating a more user-friendly experience, making it easier for visitors to navigate and engage with the site.
